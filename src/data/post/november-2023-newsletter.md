Greetings,

We are looking forward to our studio recitals for students in hour-long groups lessons and our recitals at the Lawrence Arts Center for students in private lessons this December.

### Group Lesson Recitals

All students enrolled in hour-long Group lessons will have a recital in December at Lawrence Piano Studio. The recitals will be held between Dec. 11th and Dec. 14th. Exact recital dates and times will be written on each student's assignment sheet beginning Nov. 19th.

All studio recitals are held during the regularly scheduled lesson times and begin 15 minutes after the start of the normal class time. We will use the first 15 minutes of the hour to warm up as a class. Students should arrive at their regular lesson time the day of the recital.

We will be offering a Zoom connection for any relatives, siblings, grandparents and friends that would like to watch the recital remotely. We will be sending Zoom invitations by email the week before the recital. This invitation will be sent to parents of each student. Parents may then forward the invitation to anyone they would like to attend remotely.

There will be a brief reception following these recitals.

Students enrolled in private lessons will have a recital at the Lawrence Arts Center.

We will be using the "My Music Staff" website for students to sign up for both practice recitals as well as the Winter Recital. Parents can then log-in to the "My Music Staff" site and register for both practice recitals and the Winter Recital. The email will include instructions on how to register for recitals.

We will be having a reception following the Winter Recital. The reception will be in the lower lobby of the Lawrence Arts Center, immediately following the recital.

We will send an email with your exact recital dates and times for your teacher along with instructions on how to sign up for the recitals by logging in to My Music Staff.

Please let me know if you have any questions about the recitals.

### Practice Tips

Try to play your Recital Pieces 4 times each day. Play your piece the first 2 times at half speed. Then play the piece one time up to tempo. Play any measures where you made a mistake 3 times. Then play the piece one more time.

**Memorization Tip:** When first playing your piece from memory leave the music on the music rack. Play the piece watching your hands. The music will be right there for easy reference without moving your hands.

**Recital Tip:** The more you perform a piece the easier it is the next time. Try to play for friends or family members at least once a week. For each new piece I learn, I also have to figure out what to think about while I play the piece from memory.

Practice and perform often,
